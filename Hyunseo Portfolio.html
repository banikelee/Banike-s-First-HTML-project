1. Jeon

Jeon is a traditional Korean pancake made with flour, eggs, and vegetables or meat. It is crispy on the outside and soft on the inside.

Ingridients:

  • Flour (or Korean pancake mix)
  • Eggs
  • Vegetables (green onions, onions, etc.)
  • Meat or seafood (optional)
  • Cooking oil

How to Make Jeon:

  1. Wash and cut the vegetables into small pieces.
  2. If using meat or seafood, cut it into small pieces.
  3. Put flour (or pancake mix) in a bowl.
  4. Add eggs and water.
  5. Mix everything well to make a smooth batter.
  6. Add the vegetables and meat into the batter.
  7. Heat a pan on medium heat.
  8. Add cooking oil to the pan.
  9. Pour the batter into the pan and spread it evenly.
  10. Cook for about 3–4 minutes.
  11. Flip the pancake carefully.
  12. Cook the other side until golden brown.
  13. Take it out and cut into pieces.

2. seasonla Namul

Namul is a Korean side dish made with seasonal vegetables. It is lightly seasoned and healthy.

Ingridients:

  • Seasonal vegetables (spinach, bracken, etc.)
  • Garlic
  • Sesame oil
  • Soy sauce or salt
  • Sesame seeds

How to Make Jeon:

  1. Wash the vegetables carefully.
  2. Boil water in a pot.
  3. Put the vegetables into the boiling water.
  4. Blanch them for 1–3 minutes.
  5. Take them out and put them in cold water.
  6. Squeeze out the extra water.
  7. Cut the vegetables into bite-sized pieces if needed.
  8. Put them in a bowl.
  9. Add minced garlic.
  10. Add sesame oil.
  11. Add soy sauce or salt.
  12. Mix everything well with your hands or a spoon.
  13. Sprinkle sesame seeds on top.
  14. Serve as a side dish.
